Sipag App: Project Overview

Mission

What problem does this project address?
Based on a study conducted by Crisanto (2022), the current task-managing or productivity-tracking applications available in the market fail to meet the feature requirements prioritized by the faculty and staff of the University of the Philippines Open University (UPOU) for effective planning and productivity tracking. Therefore, the project aims to propose a potential solution to address the feature requirements that the majority of the intended users need, in order to fill the gap and provide a system that can meet their requirements.
What is the objective of this project?
Sipag is a web-based application that serves as an efficient and beneficial task-management and productivity-tracking tool for the UPOU community. The objective of this project is to evaluate the usefulness of the final system and enhance it through an iterative approach.
What are some key features that define the product?
  • Includes features that the UPOU community deems essential.
  • Familiar and effective user interface.
  • AI enabled features.
  • Cross-platform and synchornized across devices.
What makes this product different from others?
Sipag is a web-based planner and productivity tracker that aligns with the practices of faculty and staff of the University of the Philippines Open University (UPOU) for planning and productivity tracking. The proposed system has a unique selling point as it is based on a research-based approach that akes it more effective in planning and productivity tracking for the UPOU community. Future studies could expand on this research-based approach to designing information systems for other communities, making them more effective tools for planning and productivity tracking.

Scope

What is the scope of this project?
The scope of this project is to build a web application called Sipag (provisionally) that is based on industry best practices and standards. The goal is to strategically plan and execute the development of a production-grade application that will be beneficial to the UPOU community.
What are the high-level assumptions or ground rules for the project?
  • It is a assumed that the proponent is engaged in important activities related to their professional and personal life and may necessitate the deprioritation of this project that will possibly push the official release date.
  • It is assumed that the project will comply users' data privacy rights.
  • It is assumed that the application users will do their best of protect their data privacy.
  • Ground Rules:
    1. Be AGILE
    2. Test driven development. Test early and often
    3. Always apply SOLID principles
    4. Keep it DRY
    5. YAGNI
What are we not going to do?
This project will not attempt to compete in the software market. It will be developed in the hope that it will be a useful tool for the UPOU community.
Is this project part of a larger effort?
No, this project is not part of a larger effort.
